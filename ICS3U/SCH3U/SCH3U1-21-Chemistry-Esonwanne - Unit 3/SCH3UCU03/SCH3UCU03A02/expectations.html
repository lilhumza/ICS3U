Expectations

Overall Expectations:

SIV.01 demonstrate scientific investigation skills (related to both inquiry and research) in the four areas of skills (initiating and planning, performing and recording, analysing and interpreting, and communicating);
QCV.02 investigate quantitative relationships of the mole concept and its significance to the quantitative analysis of chemical reactions;
QCV.03 demonstrate an understanding of the mole concept and its significance to the quantitative analysis of chemical reactions.

Specific Expectations:

SI1.12 use appropriate numeric, symbolic, and graphic modes of representation, and appropriate units of measurement (e.g., SI and imperial units);
QC2.01 use appropriate terminology related to quantities in chemical reactions,
including, but not limited to: stoichiometry, percentage yield, mole, and atomic
mass;
QC2.03 solve problems related to quantities in chemical reactions by performing
calculations involving quantities in moles, number of particles, and atomic mass;
QC3.02 describe the relationships between Avogadro’s number, the mole concept, and the molar mass of any given substance.

Catholic Graduate Expectations:

CGE2b reads, understands and uses written materials effectively;
CGE2d writes and speaks fluently one or both of Canada’s official languages.
