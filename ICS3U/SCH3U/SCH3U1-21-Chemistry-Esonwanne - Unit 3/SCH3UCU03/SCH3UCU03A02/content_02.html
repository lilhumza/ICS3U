  2. How many moles of copper atoms can be found in 8.4 x 1023 atoms of copper?

    Answer

As stated, 1 mole of the carbon-12 isotope contains 6.022 x 1023 atoms. Also, 1 mole of the carbon-12 isotope has a mass (m) of 12.00 grams. The relationship between the mass per mole of an element is known as the molar mass (M) and it is equal to its relative atomic mass. The relative atomic mass of carbon-12 is 12.00 u. The molar mass of carbon-12 is 12.00 g/mol.

When chemists are measuring the mass of an element, such as copper, for a particular purpose and the number of moles are important, the formula showing the mathematical relationship between the mass (m) of an element, its molar mass (M) and the number of moles (n) of the element is  m = n x M
