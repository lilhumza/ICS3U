Expectations

Overall Expectations:

SIV.01 demonstrate scientific investigation skills (related to both inquiry and research) in the four areas of skills (initiating and planning, performing and recording, analysing and interpreting, and communicating);
SIV.02 identify and describe careers related to the fields of science under study, and describe the contributions of scientists, including Canadians, to those fields;
QCV.01 analyse processes in the home, the workplace, and the environmental sector that use chemical quantities and calculation, and assess the importance of quantitative accuracy in industrial chemical processes;
QCV.02 investigate quantitative relationships in chemical reactions, and solve related problems;
QCV.03 demonstrate an understanding of the mole concept and its significance to the quantitative analysis of chemical reactions.

Specific Expectations:

QC1.01 analyse processes in the home, the workplace, and the environmental sector that involve the use of chemical quantities and calculations (e.g., mixing household cleaning solutions, calculating chemotherapy doses, monitoring pollen counts);
QC1.02 assess, on the basis of research, the importance of quantitative accuracy in industrial chemical processes and the potential impact on the environment if quantitative accuracy is not observed;
QC2.01 use appropriate terminology related to quantities in chemical reactions, including, but not limited to: stoichiometry, percentage yield, limiting reagent, mole, and atomic mass;
QC2.03 solve problems related to quantities in chemical reactions by performing calculations involving quantities in moles, number of particles, and atomic mass;
QC3.02 describe the relationships between Avogadro’s number, the mole concept , and the molar mass of any given substance;
QC30.4 explain the quantitative relationships expressed in a balanced chemical equation, using appropriate units of measure (e.g., moles, grams, atoms, ions, molecules);
SI1.11  use appropriate numeric (e.g. SI and imperial units), symbolic, and graphic modes of representation;
SI1.06 compile accurate data from laboratory and other sources, and organize and record the data, using appropriate formats, including tables, flow charts, graphs, and/or diagrams;
SI1.09 analyse the information gathered from research sources for logic, accuracy, reliability, adequacy, and bias;
SI1.12 express the results of any calculations involving data accurately and precisely, to the appropriate number of decimal places or significant figures;
SI2.01 identify and describe a variety of careers related to the field of science under study (e.g., pharmacist, quality assurance technologist, forensic scientist, chemical engineer, science writer, food scientist, environmental chemist, occupational health and safety officer, water quality analyst, chemical and drug sales representative, atmospheric scientist, meteorologist) and the education and training necessary for these careers.
