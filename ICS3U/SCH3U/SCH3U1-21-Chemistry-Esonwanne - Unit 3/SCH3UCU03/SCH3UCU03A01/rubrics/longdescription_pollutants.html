Unit 3: Quantities in Chemical Reactions

Activity 1: Reactions, Society and the Environment

Long Description of Rubric

Monitoring Pollutants

Category: Knowledge and Understanding, identifies the relevant chemical and physical properties of the pollutant and the industrial reaction(s) producing this pollutant. QCV1.02
Criteria: Level 1. Demonstrates limited knowledge of the relevant chemical and physical properties of the pollutant and the industrial reaction(s) producing this pollutant.
Criteria: Level 2, (60-69%). Demonstrates some  knowledge of the relevant chemical and physical properties of the pollutant and the industrial reaction(s) producing this pollutant.
Criteria: Level 3, (70-79%). Demonstrates considerable knowledge of the relevant chemical and physical properties of the pollutant and the industrial reaction(s) producing this pollutant.
Criteria: Level 4, (80-100%). Demonstrates a high degree of knowledge of the relevant chemical and physical properties of the pollutant and the industrial reaction(s) producing this pollutant.

Category: Communication, communication of information, analysis and proposal. SI1.11
Criteria: Level 1, (50-59%). Expresses and organizes information, analysis and ideas with limited clarity and purpose.
Criteria: Level 2, (60-69%). Expresses and organizes information, analysis and ideas with some clarity and purpose.
Criteria: Level 3, (70-79%). Expresses and organizes information, analysis and ideas with considerable clarity and purpose.
Criteria: Level 4, (80-100-expresses and organizes information, analysis and ideas with a high degree of clarity and purpose.

Category: Application, analyzes the importance of accurately monitoring the pollutant’s levels and subsequent effects on human health and environment. QC2.01
Criteria: Level 1, (50-59%). Analyzes the importance of accurately monitoring the pollutant’s levels and subsequent effects on human health and environment with limited effectiveness.
Criteria: Level 2, (60-69%). Analyzes the importance of accurately monitoring the pollutant’s levels and subsequent effects on human health and environment with some effectiveness.
Criteria: Level 3, (70-79%). Analyzes the importance of accurately monitoring the pollutant’s levels and subsequent effects on human health and environment with considerable effectiveness.
Criteria: Level 4, (80-100%). Analyzes the importance of accurately monitoring the pollutant’s levels and subsequent effects on human health and environment with a high degree of effectiveness.

Note: A student whose achievement is below Level 1 (50%) has not met the expectations for this assignment or activity.
