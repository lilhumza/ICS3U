Unit Review

The following main areas of the unit should be reviewed before taking the unit test:

  • Knowledge of the terms:  stoichiometry, percentage yield, empirical formula, molecular formula, mole, Avogadro’s number, formula unit, molar mass, limiting reagent, percentage composition and law of definite proportions; and
  • Calculations involving: molar mass, Avogadro’s number, moles, empirical formula, molecular formula, limiting reagents, percentage yield, theoretical yield, and stoichiometry.

Consider the following questions as a review for the unit. Complete them and submit to the dropbox in the next tab under assignment.

  1. Find the number of carbon atoms in 2.5 mol. (1 mark)

 

  1. A piece of rust contains 1.88 x 1022 atoms.  Find the number of moles in rust. (1 mark)

 

  1. Find the number of formula units in 5 g of Fe2O3. (2 marks)

 

  1. Find the molecular mass of Si(OH)4. (1 mark)

 

  1. Find the percentage of carbon, by mass, in aspirin, C9H8O4. (2 marks)

 

  1. During World War I, one of the poisonous gases used in chemical warfare was phosgene.  It was composed of 12.1 % carbon, 16.2 % oxygen and  71.7 % chlorine.  Find the empirical formula of phosgene. (2 marks)

 

  1. Determine the molecular formula of vitamin C that shows the following 7 composition, by mass:  40.9% C, 4.6% H, and 54.5% O.  Its molecular molar mass is 176.14 g/mol. (3 marks)

 

  1. Use the formula for water to explain the law of definite proportions. (2 marks)

 

  1. Find the mass of carbon dioxide formed when 50 g of propane reacts with an excess amount of oxygen gas according to the balanced equation: (4 marks)

C3H8     +    5O2       3CO2    +    4H2O

 

  1. Ammonia reacts with oxygen gas to produce nitrous oxide and water. One of the uses of nitrous oxide is in the preparation of whipped cream. The following reaction occurs: (7 marks)

4NH3(g)    +    5O2(aq)         4NO(g)    +    6H2O(l)

 

Find the percentage yield of NO if only 175 g are produced when 100.0 g of ammonia reacts with 250 g of oxygen gas.
