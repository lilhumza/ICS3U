Content

Under standard conditions of temperature, 274 K, and pressure, 101.3 kPa, one mole of any ideal gas occupies a volume of 22.4 L. This is called the standard molar volume.

1 mol of an ideal gas = 22.4 L of gas (at STP)

Example

An idling, poorly tuned car can emit 280.1 g of carbon monoxide into the atmosphere in 10 minutes. Calculate the volume of gas emitted in those ten minutes based on 101.3 kPa of pressure and a temperature of 25°C.
