 Tips

These are the steps for writing the formulas for binary ionic compounds:

    1. Recognize the metal and non-metal combination in the binary compound.
    2. Write down the symbol of the elements and their respective oxidation numbers in the top right hand corner.  Note:  You have not yet written the answer. 
    3. Place the symbols, and their respective oxidation numbers, beside each other.  The metal is always to the left.
    4. Crossover the oxidation numbers, minus the + and – signs, from one element to the other element.

Example

The Crossover  Rule: Beryllium phosphide

Step One:  Metal is beryllium and the non-metal is phosphorus

Step Two:  The respective symbols are Be2+ and As3-.

Step Three:  Be2+, As3-.

Step Four:  Be3As2

Compounds with Polyatomic Ions

Ionic compounds that contain polyatomic ions consist of two main groups within the compound:  metal ion and a polyatomic ion.  Each of these two components contains a charge.  The polyatomic ions are also known as radicals.

Hydrates

There are some compounds that have one or more water molecules weakly bonded to the formula unit of the salt.  These hydrates’ formulas show a number ratio between the salt and the water molecule. In naming the compound, the name of the salt comes first, followed by a prefix denoting the number of water molecules, and hydrate.  Examine the following example:

Example

CuSO4•5H2O  is copper (II) sulphate pentahydrate or cupric sulphate pentahydrate

Molecular Compounds

Molecular compounds are also known as covalent compounds.  These compounds consist of a non-metal bonded with a non-metal.  Non-metals are found to the right of the staircase in the Periodic Table. 

Binary Compounds and Nomenclature

Molecular compounds consisting of only two elements are known as binary compounds.  Prefixes are used to indicate the number of atoms of each element in the formula of a molecular compound and “ide” ending still applies in the names.

 Tips

The following prefixes are used:

Prefix Means Prefix Means
mono- 1 hexa- 6
di- 2 hepta- 7
tri- 3 octa- 8
tetra- 4 nona- 9
penta- 5 deca- 10

      Note: 

    • If the prefix is followed by a vowel, the final “a” or “o” is dropped.
    • The prefix “mono-“ is not used for the first element.
