Long Description

Chart 1

General Formula: A represents the central atom and B represents 2 attached atoms.
Number of Non-bonded Electron Pairs:  None
Lewis Structure: Atom B has 2 lines representing 2 bonds on each side to the central atom A on both sides.
Name of Shape: Linear
Three Dimensional Diagram of Molecule: 2 atoms on either side of the central atom forming a molecule that is said to be linear.

General Formula: A represents the central atom and B represents 2 the attached atoms.
Number of Non-bonded Electron Pairs: 2 pairs of electrons above the atom A
Lewis Structure: Central atom A has 2 B atoms on each side.
Each Atom B has 1 line representing 1 bond to the central atom A. But the 2 bonds are bent away in the same direction.
Name of Shape: Angular
Three Dimensional Diagram of Molecule: 2 atoms (B) on either side of the central atom bending away from the atom A, on the same direction is said to be Angular.

General Formula: A represents the central atom and B represents 3 the attached atoms.
Number of Non-bonded Electron Pairs: 1 pair of electrons above the atom A
Lewis Structure: Central atom A has 3 B atoms.
Name of Shape: Pyramidal
Three Dimensional Diagram of Molecule: Central atom A has 3 B atoms. All Bs are bending away from the atom A, in the same direction is said to be Pyramidal.

General Formula: A represents the central atom and B represents 4 attached atoms.
Number of Non-bonded Electron Pairs: None
Lewis Structure: Central atom A has 4 B atoms.
Name of Shape: Tetrahedral
Three Dimensional Diagram of Molecule: Central atom A has 4 B atoms. On any one side, 3Bs are always bending away from the atom A. While remaining B atom points in the opposite direction to 3 other B atoms.
