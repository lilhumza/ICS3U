Section A

In molecular compounds where there is a central atom in the Lewis diagram, the shape of the molecule is dependent on the number of bonds and the number of non-bonded pairs of electrons surrounding the central atom.

Given a series of molecular compounds, use the data in Table 1 as a reference, to complete the necessary information for each compound. The Lewis diagram is the starting point for determining the number of non-bonded electron pairs, shape and the three dimensional diagram of the molecule.
