Chemicals Are in the House!

Before you begin reading the article below, read through this entire page.


Please read The Toxic House and then continue on with the remainder of the content page. You may also be able to access an interesting interactive learning object created by the CBC. Your ability to access this learning object will depend on your flash connection.

Journal


As you read The Toxic House, make notes in your journal addressing the “Before” and “During” activities.  Answer the “After” questions once you have finished reading the article.

Before

  1. Decide on an area of the house that interests you.
  2. List the major items in your chosen area of the house that are featured in “Chemicals Are in the House.”
  3. Examine the heading and the subheadings given for the item.
  4. Read the “Overview’, “Problems” and “Solutions.”
  5. Write down what you already know about the items containing the chemicals and the chemical/products themselves.
  6. Record any questions that you might have about the topic.

During

  1. Under the headings “Problems” and “Solutions” write down brief point-form notes on important and interesting information.
  2. Write down any thoughts about the information that you have written down.

After

  1. Go to other sources to check the accuracy and reliability of the information that you have written down.
  2. Make sure that you keep track of the sources that you use for your research.
