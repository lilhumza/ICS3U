Decomposition Reactions

Can table sugar be turned into carbon?

The reactant is table sugar, C12H22O11. The products were elemental carbon and water. Sulphuric acid was added to the sugar, but it is not a product or a reactant. The sugar was decomposed into two substances and if the conditions were right, the water molecules could have decomposed into hydrogen and oxygen. This leads us to the idea that a decomposition reaction involves one reactant decomposing into separate constituents.

The General Formula for a Decomposition Reaction is

AB             A      +      B     

Does the general formula for a decomposition reaction look familiar?  The decomposition reaction is the reverse of the synthesis/combination reaction.
