Chemical Equations

Did you know?

When you are making toast and you burn some of the bread, you produce carbon atoms. Carbon in its elemental form is a black solid. With high enough temperatures, the carbon in the bread will react with the oxygen in the air and produce carbon dioxide. However, when there is not enough oxygen to react with the carbon, then carbon monoxide is a product of the reaction.

Did you know?

An automobile engineer has to figure out how much air should be mixed with a certain amount of gasoline to efficiently run an engine. Chemical reactions play an important role in both energy conservation and increased efficiency in operations of engines and machines, two critical issues today.

Chemical Word Equations

People speak in very common terms when describing chemical reactions like burning marshmallows or mixing vinegar with baking soda. But these terms are also the starting point for more technical descriptions of chemical reactions. The chemical(s) involved have names and formulas that can be translated into a reaction, showing the atoms of the elements and illustrating how they rearrange themselves to produce new substances. Knowledge of nomenclature, charges of atoms and formulas are very important in writing chemical word equations.
