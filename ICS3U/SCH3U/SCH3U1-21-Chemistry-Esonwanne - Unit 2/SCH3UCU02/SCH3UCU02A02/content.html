Chemical Reactions

A chemical reaction is an event that describes specific changes in substances. The components of a chemical reaction can be subdivided and presented in the following organizational map:

Chemical Equations:

Chemical reactions are represented using chemical equations. There are three basic components to a chemical equation: reactants, products and the “arrow”. The general presentation is as follows:

Reactant(s)     Product(s)

The reactant side of the chemical equation consists of the substance(s) that is/are undergoing a chemical change and are positioned to the left side of the arrow. The product(s) is/are the new substance(s) created in the chemical change and are located to the right of the arrow. The arrow represents the division between the reactant(s) and the product(s).

States of Matter in a Chemical Equation:

In many cases, the states of matter of the reactants and products in a chemical equation are important. Sometimes, the states of matter are not included in the chemical equation for a particular reason. In those cases, the states of matter are not needed in the context of the information that the chemical equation is trying to convey i.e., stating the substances undergoing the chemical change irrespective of their states.

If the states of matter are used in the chemical equation then they are indicated by a respective bracketed code written to the right of the formula. They are as follows:

 Solid - (s)
Liquid - (l)
Gas - (g)
Aqueous - (aq)
Another clue to indicate the state of matter, especially for an element, is the term “elemental state”. If an element is in its elemental state, then the code beside the symbol reflects the state of the element at room temperature. As an example, copper is a solid at room temperature and so the code for its symbol is Cu(s). Copper in liquid form is not in its elemental state. The term “elemental state” only applies to elements.

Aluminum is a solid at room temperature and its code would show a solid state. Helium is a gas at room temperature and so its code would show gaseous state. Oxygen gas is a gas at room temperature and so its code should show a (g). Silver is a solid at room temperature and so its code should show a (s).

Precipitates

Whenever two clear liquids mix together and solids form it is called a precipitation reaction. The solid is referred to as a precipitate. The code is written beside the formula in a chemical equation is (s) for solid.
