Energy in a Chemical Equation

Chemical reactions can be classified into two categories in terms of the energy released or absorbed:  exothermic or endothermic. A common symbol for energy in a chemical equation is Δ.

Watch or listen to the video the clip entitled, Exothermic Reaction - Glycerin and Permanganate Torch. 

The chemical reaction that releases more energy than it needed to get started, is called an exothermic reaction.

The chemical reaction that absorbs more energy than it needed to react is called an endothermic reaction.

Acids and Bases: Nomenclature

Other types of matter that are very common in chemical reactions are acids and bases. It is important to know the formulas and respective names of acids and bases involved in chemical reactions. The nomenclature of acids and bases can be divided into two camps:  binary acids and oxyacids, but please note that compounds are acids when in an aqueous solution.

Bases
Bases consist of a metal and the hydroxide ion, OH-. The name is composed of two parts:  meta  +  hydroxide.

Bases Nomenclature

Here are some examples:

    NaOH  is sodium hydroxide
    Ca(OH)2  is calcium hydroxide
    Li(OH)  is lithium hydroxide
    Al(OH)3 is aluminum hydroxide
