Activity 2: It’s a Mixing Thing

Answer

  1. Referring back to the solubility graph, how much precipitate of ammonium chloride, NH4Cl, would form if the saturated solution cooled from 90°C to 50°C?

20 g of ammonium chloride would precipitate out.

Explanation:  At 90°C, the saturated solution contains 70g and then when it cools to 50°C, the solubility is 50 g.  The difference is the 20g of the precipitate.
