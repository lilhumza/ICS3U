Chemical Contaminants in Tested Drinking Water

Municipal drinking water is the result of a series of physical and chemical treatments that allow for human consumption. Common chemical treatments include chlorination, ozonation and fluoridation. Certain chemicals are present in drinking water due to treatment or lack of treatment and despite best efforts to eliminate all contaminants, some substances remain. Chlorine is present in drinking water where chlorination is used as a part of the treatment of water. In some cases, a contaminant is present because of older plumbing procedures. Lead soldering in the piping systems of older homes and buildings for instance, produces lead in drinking water. Quality control within the water treatment plant is strictly adhered to ensure that the contaminants' concentration levels are below government guidelines and standards. So the water you consume is still a mixture of many invisible substances.

Questions:

  1. Convert the given concentration units from g/L to ppm and ppb, and show a sample calculation of one contaminant.
  2. Research and record the maximum acceptable concentration for each contaminant.
  3. Reason for the presence of contaminant in the drinking water supply. One of the tested substances is not considered a contaminant but an additive. Identify this substance and give a reason for its addition to the drinking water supply in some municipalities.
